Hospital Emergency Room — Consolidated Performance & Patient Analytics Dashboard


Monthly Views

In April 2024, the Hospital Emergency Room treated a total of 469 patients with an average wait time of 35.0 minutes and a patient satisfaction score of 4.63 out of 5. Out of these, 217 patients were admitted representing 46.27% while 252 were not admitted at 53.73%. Only 57.1% of patients were seen within the 30-minute target, meaning 42.86% missed the benchmark. The 20 to 29 age group was the most frequent visitor with 71 patients. Female patients slightly outnumbered males at 51.39% and White patients were the largest racial group at 147. The no-referral group dominated department referrals at 284, followed by General Practice at 92 and Orthopedics at 44. Peak patient load was observed on Monday and Tuesday during early morning and mid-morning hours.

Patients Details View

The Patients Details dashboard provides individual-level records for all patients treated between April 2023 and October 2024, capturing nine key fields including Patient ID, Name, Gender, Age, Admission Date, Race, Wait Time, Department Referral, and Admission Status. Patient ages ranged from as young as 3 years to as old as 74 years with the majority of visible records being female patients. Wait times varied significantly from a minimum of 11 minutes to a maximum of 60 minutes, with longer waits generally associated with General Practice and Orthopedics referrals. Most patients arrived with no department referral, confirming a strong walk-in trend. The total combined age across all records was 367,305 and the total cumulative wait time reached 324,955 minutes, reflecting the enormous volume of care delivered over this period.

Consolidated View — Full Period

The Consolidated View presents the complete picture of ER performance from April 2023 to October 2024, covering a total of 9,216 patients with an average wait time of 35.3 minutes and an exceptional patient satisfaction score of 4.99 out of 5. Admissions were nearly equal with 4,612 patients admitted at 50.04% and 4,604 not admitted at 49.96%, demonstrating effective and balanced triage. However, 40.68% of patients missed the 30-minute wait target, which remains the most significant operational concern. The largest referral source was no referral at 5,400 patients, followed by General Practice at 1,800 and Orthopedics at 1,000. Racially, White patients led at 2,600 followed by African American at 2,000 and Two or More Races at 1,600. The heatmap showed peak pressure on Friday and Saturday evenings between 19:00 and 22:00 with some slots reaching 127 patients.

Conclusion

Across all three dashboards, the Hospital Emergency Room demonstrates strong overall performance with a near-perfect satisfaction score of 4.99, balanced admission decisions, and consistent service delivery across a diverse population over an 18-month period. However, the recurring failure to meet the 30-minute wait target, seen in both the monthly view at 42.86% and the consolidated view at 40.68%, highlights a persistent operational gap that requires immediate attention. The heavy reliance on walk-in patients without referrals places continuous unplanned pressure on the ER, and peak hours on Friday and Saturday evenings further strain resources. To elevate this facility to world-class standards, strategic investment in staffing during peak hours, stronger community referral pathways, and smarter patient flow management will be essential going forward.
