This project is an interactive Healthcare Analytics Dashboard designed to monitor and analyze emergency room performance on a monthly basis. It provides a comprehensive overview of patient flow, wait times, satisfaction levels, and operational efficiency, enabling hospitals to make data-driven decisions for better patient care.

The emergency room handled a total of 469 patients in April 2024, indicating a steady patient inflow and consistent demand for emergency services. The average wait time of 35 minutes reflects moderate operational efficiency, though there is still scope for reducing delays during peak hours.

The patient satisfaction score of 4.63⭐ highlights a strong performance in terms of service quality, staff responsiveness, and overall patient experience. Additionally, 185 patients were referred to specialized departments, showing effective triaging and coordination between the emergency unit and other hospital


📊 Patient & Operational Analysis

The admission data shows that 46.27% of patients were admitted, while 53.73% were treated and discharged, indicating that most cases were not critical and could be managed within the emergency room itself. This reflects efficient handling of minor and moderate cases without overburdening hospital beds.

From a wait-time perspective, 57.1% of patients were attended within 30 minutes, which is a positive indicator of service responsiveness. However, 42.8% of patients experienced delays, suggesting the need for better resource allocation, especially during high-traffic hours.

Demographic analysis reveals that young adults (20–29 age group) contribute the highest number of visits, which may indicate lifestyle-related emergencies or higher activity levels. Gender distribution is almost balanced, ensuring no significant bias in patient inflow.

The referral analysis shows that a majority of cases (284 patients) required no external department support, meaning the ER is capable of handling most situations independently. However, departments like General Practice and Orthopedics receive a notable share of referrals, highlighting common medical needs.

Time-based trends further reveal that Tuesdays and morning/evening hours experience peak patient volumes, which can help hospital management in optimizing staff scheduling, resource planning, and workload distribution.

📊 Overall Performance at a Glance
The Hospital Emergency Room recorded an impressive total of 9,216 patients 🧑‍⚕️ between April 2023 and October 2024, demonstrating the immense pressure modern emergency facilities operate under. Despite this overwhelming volume, the hospital managed to maintain an average wait time of just 35.3 minutes ⏱️ — a commendable achievement in emergency healthcare. Most remarkably, the patient satisfaction score soared to 4.99 out of 5 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐, reflecting exceptional dedication from the medical staff and support teams. Furthermore, 3,816 patients were successfully referred 🔄 to specialized departments, ensuring every patient received the most appropriate level of care for their condition.

👥 Patient Demographics & Admission Insights
The admissions data tells a fascinating story of balance — 4,612 patients (50.04%) were admitted 🛏️ while 4,604 patients (49.96%) were treated and released 🚶, showing an almost perfect 50/50 split. Age-wise, the ER served patients across all generations 👶👨👴, with the 10–19 age group being the most frequent visitors (1,179 patients) and numbers remaining consistently high across all brackets. Gender distribution leaned slightly towards females at 51.05% 👩 over males at 48.69% 👨, with a small non-conforming group also represented 🏳️‍🌈. Racially, White patients led at 2.6K, followed closely by African Americans at 2.0K and Two or More Races at 1.6K 🌍, highlighting the diverse community this ER proudly serves.

⏰ Timing, Referrals & Critical Challenges
The referral and timing data uncovers both strengths and urgent areas for improvement 🚨. A striking 5,400 patients arrived with no prior referral 🚪, confirming the ER as the first point of contact for thousands in crisis, while General Practice sent 1,800 patients 🩺 and Orthopedics contributed 1,000 referrals 🦴. However, the most pressing concern is that 40.68% of patients missed the 30-minute wait target ❌⏳ — meaning nearly 4 in 10 patients waited longer than acceptable standards. The day-and-hour heatmap 🗓️🔥 clearly shows that Friday and Saturday evenings between 19:00–22:00 experience the heaviest footfall, with numbers spiking to 127 patients in some slots — a loud call for smarter staffing strategies 👨‍💼📋 and resource allocation during peak hours to protect both patient outcomes and staff wellbeing 💪🏥.
